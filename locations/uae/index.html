UAE — Frequently Asked Questions

Do you serve customers in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah and the other Emirates? +

Yes — every emirate. Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, Ajman, Ras Al Khaimah, Fujairah and Umm Al Quwain. The work is done over the internet, so your location inside the UAE doesn’t affect speed or price. Customers in JLT, Al Reem Island and Al Nahda all get the same workflow.

Can I pay in AED? +

Yes. We accept AED via direct bank transfer to UAE banks (Emirates NBD, ADCB, FAB, Mashreq, RAKBANK and others), PayPal billed in AED, Wise, or international card. The quote is locked in AED so there are no FX surprises. Payment only happens after the fix is verified on your device.

Do you communicate in English or Arabic? +

Both. WhatsApp and Telegram chat works in English (default) or Arabic on request. Voice calls in Arabic can be arranged for complex cases. Written quotes and instructions are in English unless you specifically ask for Arabic, in which case we provide both.

Which devices are most common among UAE customers? +

Samsung Galaxy (S, A, Z Fold and Flip series) is the most common, followed by OnePlus, Google Pixel, Xiaomi (Mi, Redmi, POCO), Honor and Oppo. We see a lot of grey-market and parallel-imported devices brought in via Dragon Mart and Naif Souk that need region/firmware fixes — we handle those daily.

Does the service work with Etisalat or du SIMs and any carrier branding? +

Yes. We’ve worked with devices on Etisalat, du and Virgin Mobile UAE without issue. If your phone is carrier-branded or carrier-locked, we can usually debrand it and apply a clean firmware build during the same job. Carrier-locked unlock requests are also possible — we’ll quote separately based on the model.
