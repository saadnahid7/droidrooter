USA — Frequently Asked Questions

Do you serve customers across all 50 US states? +

Yes — every state. The work is done over the internet via WhatsApp or Telegram, so your location inside the US (New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Houston, Miami, Seattle, Denver, anywhere) does not affect speed or price. The only requirement is a stable Wi-Fi or data connection on your phone.

How does the price compare to a US repair shop or carrier? +

Typically 50–80% lower than uBreakiFix, Best Buy Geek Squad, or carrier in-store fees, with no shipping and no week-long waits. A typical FRP bypass or root job runs $30–$80 in USD vs. $120–$300+ in a US shop. You pay only after the fix is verified working on your device.

How do I pay from the US? +

PayPal (USD), Wise, Zelle to a partner account, or international card. Cash App is supported for smaller jobs. Quote is locked in USD so there are no FX surprises. Payment only happens after the fix is confirmed on your device — never upfront for the full amount.

Which devices and US carriers do you support? +

All major brands sold in the US: Samsung Galaxy (S, A, Z Fold, Note), Google Pixel (1 through 9 series), OnePlus, Motorola (Edge, G, Razr), and Xiaomi/POCO imports. Carriers covered: Verizon, T-Mobile, AT&T, US Cellular, Cricket, Mint Mobile, Visible, Google Fi. Carrier-locked unlock is also possible — quoted separately.

Is rooting or unlocking my Android legal in the US? +

Yes. The DMCA exemption renewed in 2021 explicitly allows US owners to root, unlock the bootloader, and install custom firmware on devices they own. Carrier unlocking is also legal under the Unlocking Consumer Choice and Wireless Competition Act. We do not work on stolen or financed devices and verify ownership before starting.
