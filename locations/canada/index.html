Local Context — Why It Matters

The Canadian Android market is dominated by three carriers (Bell, Rogers, Telus) and their flanker brands (Fido, Koodo, Virgin Plus, Public Mobile, Freedom). Each ships customised firmware on Samsung, Pixel and Motorola devices that complicates rooting, unlocking and ROM flashing. A generic global service often misses these regional builds; we don\u2019t.

Canada — Frequently Asked Questions

Do you serve customers across all Canadian provinces? +

Yes — every province and territory. Toronto, Montreal, Vancouver, Calgary, Edmonton, Ottawa, Winnipeg, Halifax, St. John’s, Yellowknife — the work happens over the internet via WhatsApp or Telegram, so your location inside Canada does not affect price or turnaround.

How does the price compare to a Canadian repair shop? +

Typically 50–75% lower than Mobile Klinik, Apple-style mall kiosks, or carrier in-store labour, with no shipping and no week-long queues. A typical FRP bypass or root job runs $40–$110 CAD vs $150–$400+ in a Canadian shop. You pay only after the fix is verified working.

PayPal (CAD), Wise, Interac e-Transfer to a partner account, or international card. Quote is locked in CAD so there are no FX surprises. Payment only happens after the fix is confirmed on your device — never upfront for the full amount.
