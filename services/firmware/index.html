Stock Firmware vs Custom ROM — What You Get

Stock firmware restores your phone to the exact image the manufacturer shipped — ideal for warranty claims, fixing bootloops, recovering from a failed update, or returning a sold phone to factory state. We download from the OEM's official source (samsungmobile.com, xiaomifirmwareupdater.com, mirrors of OnePlus / Motorola / Realme firmware) and verify checksums before flashing.

Custom ROMs replace the OS with a community-maintained AOSP build — LineageOS, Pixel Experience, crDroid, EvolutionX, RisingOS, GrapheneOS. You get longer security updates, no bloatware, modern Pixel features on older hardware and far more control. We install matching firmware (vendor / boot / dtbo), the latest stable ROM build, optional Magisk root, and verify your banking and DRM apps still work.

Frequently Asked Questions

My phone is stuck in a bootloop after an OTA. Can you fix it without losing data? +

Usually yes. We boot into recovery (TWRP, OrangeFox, or stock recovery via Fastboot), pull a logcat, identify whether the cause is a corrupt system image, a Magisk conflict, or a partition mismatch, then re-flash only the broken partition. The userdata partition is preserved unless the corruption is in it specifically.

Will custom ROMs break banking, Netflix or Google Pay? +

Most modern AOSP-based ROMs (LineageOS, Pixel Experience, crDroid) pass Play Integrity Basic out of the box. For Strong integrity (some banks, Google Pay) we install Magisk + Zygisk + DenyList + Shamiko after the ROM, which works on the majority of devices. We test banking apps on your device before billing.

Can I go back to stock firmware later if I do not like the custom ROM? +

Yes — we keep your original stock backup, and the relevant Odin / Mi Flash / MSM tool firmware is always retrievable from official sources. Returning to stock takes 30–60 minutes for most devices. We do this regularly when customers want to claim warranty or sell the phone.

What is a hard brick and can you fix it? +

A hard brick means the phone will not show any sign of life — no boot, no Fastboot, no Download mode. We rescue most hard bricks via low-level methods: Qualcomm EDL with the right authentication, Mediatek Bootrom over USB, or vendor service-mode tools. Hardware-dead bricks (failed eMMC, fried PMIC) cannot be saved by software and need physical repair.

How long does a firmware flash or ROM install take? +

Stock firmware flash: 30–60 minutes. Custom ROM install (with Magisk + integrity fix): 60–120 minutes. Brick recovery via EDL or Bootrom: 2–6 hours depending on chipset and authentication requirements. We give an exact ETA in the written quote.
