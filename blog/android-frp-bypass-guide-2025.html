What is FRP (Factory Reset Protection)?

Factory Reset Protection (FRP) is a security feature introduced by Google in Android 5.1 (Lollipop) and later versions. It's designed to prevent unauthorized access to your device if it's lost, stolen, or factory reset without your permission.

How FRP Works:
  • When you set up a Google account on your device, FRP is automatically enabled
  • If someone performs a factory reset, the device will require the original Google account credentials
  • Without the correct credentials, the device remains locked and unusable
  • This prevents thieves from easily resetting and using stolen devices

FRP Protection Levels

Standard FRP
  • Basic Google account verification
  • Can be bypassed with various methods
  • Found on older Android versions
Enhanced FRP
  • Advanced security measures
  • More difficult to bypass
  • Found on newer Android versions

When Does FRP Trigger?

Understanding when FRP activates can help you avoid getting locked out of your device:

Common FRP Trigger Scenarios:

  1. Factory Reset via Settings: When you reset your device through Settings without removing your Google account first
  2. Hard Reset: Using hardware buttons to reset the device while a Google account is still linked
  3. Recovery Mode Reset: Performing a wipe data/factory reset from recovery mode
  4. Flashing Firmware: Installing new firmware without properly removing the Google account
  5. Second-hand Devices: Purchasing a used device where the previous owner didn't remove their account

Always remove your Google account from Settings > Accounts before performing any factory reset to avoid FRP activation.
